Methodology & Framework

About This Audit

How the Australian Kanon works, how it was built, and how this audit measures a real actor against it.

What this is

This is a structural audit of a public figure, currently Pauline Hanson, against the Australian Kanon: a 343-vector semantic map of the nation's identity built on the Qqci 7x7x7 framework. The audit does not ask "is this a good or bad politician." It asks a narrower, mechanical question: does this actor's documented record of quotes, votes, and positions align with or oppose each of the 343 fixed reference points that make up the Kanon's model of Australian identity.

The 343 vectors are organised as 7 Planes, each corresponding to one of the seven interrogatives (Who, What, Where, Why, How, Cause, Effect), and each Plane is further subdivided into 7 Rows and 7 vectors per Row, giving 7 x 7 x 7 = 343. Every vector carries a fixed coordinate pair (upsilon for Morality, psi for Will) set when the Kanon itself was built, independent of any individual audit. An audit never changes these coordinates; it only judges whether the actor's real, sourced conduct hits or fails the vector the coordinate describes.

An elevator pitch on relative morality

The specific numbers in the Kanon are, by admission, arbitrary. There is no cosmic law that says "systemic justice" is exactly +2.0 and "tyranny" is exactly -2.0 rather than some other pair of numbers. What isn't arbitrary is the relative range those numbers create once fixed, in the same way a ruler's decision to call one end "0cm" and the other "30cm" is arbitrary, but once fixed, it lets you say with total precision that one object is twice as long as another.

Think of the Kanon as an instrument for spectroscopy of a cultural field rather than a physical one. Ordinary spectroscopy works because a fixed set of reference lines, tied to real atomic transitions, only "lights up" when matter of a matching kind is present: like interacts with like, a photon only gets absorbed when its energy matches the transition it's hitting. The Kanon builds the same kind of instrument for the relative universe of Australian national identity: 343 fixed reference lines, each tuned to detect one specific kind of conduct (a Who-plane line reads identity-type matter, a Where-plane line reads land-type matter, and so on), and an actor's real, documented public record, speeches, votes, platform commitments, is the matter run through it. The reading at each line isn't a claim that "Australia" literally has a coordinate of (+0.6, +0.4) somewhere in physical reality; it's a claim that, within this specific constructed instrument, this specific piece of real conduct does or doesn't resonate with this specific fixed line.

The payoff of that arbitrariness is discernibility, not truth. Once the net is fixed, you can hold two different actors, institutions, or historical moments up against the exact same 343 points and see where they diverge, which is a much harder thing to do with unstructured impression or vibes-based political commentary. The Kanon doesn't claim to have found the One True Measure of Australian identity; it claims to have built one consistent measure, applied it evenly, and shown its work, so that any disagreement can be argued at the level of "is this specific vector's coordinate right" or "does this specific quote actually support this specific verdict," rather than at the level of vague, unfalsifiable vibes.

The two axes

Every vector is placed on two independent scales:

Axis upsilon (Morality) - who benefits? Runs from +2 (Systemic Justice, benefiting everyone) through +1 (Greater Good, benefiting others), 0 (neutral), -1 (Lesser Evil, benefiting one's own group only), down to -2 (Tyranny, benefiting only the self).

Axis psi (Will) - what is the energy doing? Runs from +2 (Productive Justice, actively creating systemic value) through +1 (proactive building), 0 (neutral), -1 (passive suppression or withholding), down to -2 (Chaos or Collapse, actively destroying or extracting value).

A vector's fixed coordinate describes what that point in the Kanon's structure represents when Australia is functioning as itself. The audit's job is to determine, node by node, whether the actor's actions channel that description (HIT) or invert it (FAIL).

Scoring

Each of the 343 vectors resolves to a binary verdict: HIT or FAIL. There is no partial credit; a vector either matches its Kanon description in the actor's real conduct, or it doesn't. Verdicts are never split down the middle, and a "MISS" (no evidence either way) is tracked separately and excluded from scoring rather than defaulted to either side.

The headline Alignment Score is calculated as (HITs minus FAILs) divided by the total number of scored vectors, expressed as a percentage. This is deliberately not HITs divided by total, because a HIT-heavy but FAIL-heavy record should not read as more aligned than it is; the FAILs have to be subtracted, not ignored.

Average coordinate scores are verdict-weighted, not raw. If a vector's fixed coordinate is +0.7 and the actor FAILs it, the actor's effective position on that axis is treated as -0.7, not +0.7 and not zero. A FAIL is not "no signal," it's an active inversion. Averaging the raw fixed coordinates without applying this sign-flip produces a number that looks like it is describing the Kanon's own values rather than the actor's relationship to them, which is why every score on this site is computed the verdict-weighted way.

Sourcing standard

Every Quote field in this audit is a real, verbatim, attributed quote or, where no clean single sentence exists, an explicitly-labelled documented paraphrase of a specific, real, sourced position. Nothing is invented. Coordinates in parentheses appear once, in the header line, and are never restated or paraphrased in the body prose. A node's Description, Justification, and Actuality sections each argue the case in plain language, they do not narrate the scoring mechanism, and they do not use the coordinate shorthand as a substitute for an actual argument.

Sourcing follows a strict hierarchy: primary transcripts and Hansard records first, official party platform pages and press releases second, reported speech and press coverage third. Training data is used only as a hypothesis generator, to suggest which real, specific incident a vector's mechanism probably matches, never as a citation in itself. Every hypothesis is verified against a live, fetched source before it is allowed into the document. If a search comes up empty, the correct response is to broaden the source type (Hansard, party platform, press archive, official senator's office site), not to round a near-miss up to "close enough" or to write a self-authored summary sentence with a citation bolted on afterward.

How the Kanon itself was constructed

The 343 reference points are not scored or judged, they're derived. Each of the 343 addresses is built by triangulating three coordinates at once: which Plane (Who/What/Where/Why/How/Cause/Effect), which Row within that Plane, and which of the 7 vectors within that Row. The address Who.Where.Why, for example, asks "what is the Motivation (Why) derived from the Land (Where) that shapes the Identity (Who)" and for Australia resolves to "Populate or Perish", the historical drive to fill a vast, sparsely-populated continent before some other power did it for them. Every one of the 343 addresses is derived this way, by asking what a nation's real, documented history, law, and culture would say if you triangulated those three axes honestly.

Each entry is anchored by a real, sourced canonical quote or documented event, never an invented or paraphrased placeholder. Sourcing follows a strict priority order: founders and originators of the concept first, then deeply iconic quotes embedded in national consciousness, then material with high emotional voltage (pride, shame, grief), then well-documented historical quotes more broadly, and administrative or documentary facts only as a last resort when no genuine "soul quote" exists for that vector. If no direct quote can be found meeting that bar, the vector is left unaudited rather than filled with something invented to complete the set.

Training data is used only as a hypothesis generator during construction too: given a vector's real mechanism (not its poetic Kanon name), a builder can usually guess which specific real speech, law, or event a well-documented nation is known for that matches it, and that guess is then verified against a live, checkable source before it's allowed into the Kanon as the canonical quote. A hypothesis that can't be verified this way is discarded rather than rounded up to "close enough."

Where a mainstream concept has an obscured or erased indigenous counterpart, a second "shadow" entry is added at the same address, same three-axis derivation, but its own distinct quote and its own distinct coordinates, exposing what the mainstream entry's framing leaves out. This is how, for instance, an address about "the frontier" carries both the settler mythology of expansion and a First Nations Perspective entry naming what that expansion actually cost.

Once the Kanon itself is built and fixed this way, it becomes the stable reference instrument described above, the fixed set of 343 lines, against which any individual actor's real conduct can then be audited, HIT or FAIL, vector by vector. Corrections to an audit (a fabricated quote, a verdict word that drifted out of sync with its own argument, a stale tally) are corrections to the measurement of an actor against the Kanon; they are a different layer from corrections to the Kanon's own 343 fixed reference points, which change far more rarely and only when the underlying derivation itself is judged wrong.

How this specific audit (Hanson vs. the Australian Kanon) was actually conducted

Building the Kanon and auditing an actor against it are two different jobs. The section above describes how the 343 reference points themselves were derived. This section describes the separate, ongoing process of measuring one real person, Pauline Hanson, against that already-fixed grid.

The audit runs on a two-move method applied to every one of the 343 vectors in turn. Move one is reading the vector's own address correctly: each address is three coordinates at once (Plane, Row, Vector), and the first job is to state plainly what ideal that specific triangulation is actually describing, not just its poetic Kanon name, but its underlying mechanism: who it's meant to benefit, and what kind of energy embodying it takes. Move two is finding Hanson's own real, documented mechanism on that same question, from her actual speeches, votes, and public record, and then comparing the two shapes directly rather than the two topics loosely. An actor can use the same words as an ideal while inverting what it actually does, and the audit's job is to catch that difference, not just check whether the right keywords appear.

Every quote in the audit is sourced through a strict hierarchy, applied in this order: Hansard and parliamentary transcripts first (her own Senate speeches are the highest-value source, since they're dated, attributed, and fully in context), official party platform pages and press releases second, and reported speech or press coverage third, used mainly for anything said outside the chamber. Training knowledge is allowed to generate a hypothesis about which real, specific incident a vector probably matches, "this sounds like it could be the such-and-such speech", but that hypothesis is never itself treated as a citation. It has to be verified against a directly fetched primary source before it's allowed into the document, and if the fetch doesn't confirm it, the hypothesis is dropped rather than rounded up to "close enough."

This audit is explicitly a living, corrected document rather than a single finished output. Multiple passes across this project have caught and fixed real errors in place: fabricated statistics that read plausibly but didn't match the actual source, quotes cited to the wrong document, header verdicts (HIT/FAIL) that had drifted out of sync with their own supporting argument, coordinate notation that had leaked into body prose where it doesn't belong, reused quotes that needed an explicit note rather than a silent duplicate, and plane-level score tallies that had gone stale relative to the actual node-by-node headers once earlier corrections changed individual verdicts. Each of these was caught by re-reading the actual document directly (not by trusting a prior summary of it) and fixed at the source, with the specific error and fix logged so later passes don't have to re-derive work that's already been checked. The score you see reflects the current state of that ongoing correction process, not a one-time output that's assumed correct by default.

The deeper theory: isomorphism, not identity

The framing above uses spectroscopy as a working analogy, not a claim that culture is literally physics. What's actually being claimed is an isomorphism: a structural correspondence between how spectroscopy identifies matter in the physical universe and how the Kanon identifies definition in a cultural one. An isomorphism doesn't say two things are the same thing; it says they share the same shape, the same relationships between their parts, even though what the parts are made of is completely different.

Under this view, the Kanon itself, 343 vectors, fixed and internally consistent, describes one bounded, coherent shape of "what Australia is," a single definitional structure sitting inside a much larger and messier field of possible definitions, in the same way a single species is one coherent, bounded shape within a much larger taxonomy of life. There is no claim that this is the only valid shape a nation's identity could take. Other genus of definition are possible, built by other people triangulating the same kind of question differently, and this Kanon doesn't claim to have found the one true version, only to have built one complete, consistent, evidence-anchored version and shown its work.

The "like interacts with like" principle carries over exactly, and it's the part doing the most real work: a given vector only meaningfully registers the kind of conduct it's actually tuned to. A Who-plane vector doesn't light up on land policy, a Cause-plane vector doesn't light up on rhetorical style. This is why the audit insists on matching an actor's real mechanism to a vector's real mechanism rather than matching surface topic or keyword, the resonance has to be structural, not superficial, or the reading is meaningless.

That also gives a name to what's actually being measured: not the actor directly, but a typed unit of evidence, a documented statement or action, considered specifically in its role as a piece of national-identity-defining conduct. Call it culture:matter in general, and AU-Culture:Meta-Matter when it's specifically a piece of conduct being read against the Australian Kanon. The same typing scheme generalises to any other nation or group's Kanon, which is exactly why the underlying build process supports constructing equivalent instruments for other countries: the instrument changes, but the idea of typed, isomorphic cultural matter being read against it does not.

The seven planes

  1. Identity (Who) - the metaphysical plane, will and direction.
  2. Definition (What) - the possible plane, faith and probability, the nation's constitutional and legal self-definition.
  3. Land (Where) - the physical plane, matter and distance, the continent itself.
  4. Drive (Why) - the lyrical plane, meaning and resonance, the nation's motivating myths.
  5. Method (How) - the logical plane, count and consistency, the nation's operating procedures.
  6. Foundation (Cause) - the historical plane, sequence and causality, how the nation came to be.
  7. Result (Effect) - the emotive plane, passion and consequence, what the nation's project actually produces.

Each plane is scored independently and then summed for the overall Alignment Score shown on the dashboard.
